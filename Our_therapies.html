Our Therapies

Comprehensive Therapy Programmes

At the heart of the Cerebral Palsy Foundation Centre (CPFC) lies a commitment to providing comprehensive support to our students. Central to our approach are a variety of therapy services designed to address the diverse needs of individuals with disabilities.

From Physical Therapy (PT) aimed at improving mobility and coordination, to Speech Therapy (ST) focused on enhancing communication skills, our range of modalities is carefully curated to foster holistic development. At CPFC, we recognise the unique potential of each individual and strive to provide tailored interventions that empower our students to thrive.

Physical Therapy (PT)

PT is a cornerstone of the therapy offerings at CPFC, serving individuals with physical disabilities such as cerebral palsy, spina bifida, muscular dystrophy, and orthopaedic conditions. Through targeted exercises and interventions, PT aims to improve mobility, strength, balance, and coordination, enabling students to enhance their functional independence and quality of life.

Occupational Therapy (OT)

OT plays a vital role in supporting individuals with developmental delays, autism spectrum disorder, sensory processing disorder, physical disabilities, and cognitive impairments affecting daily activities. By addressing skills related to self-care, productivity, and leisure, OT helps students develop essential life skills and overcome barriers to participation in daily routines and social interactions.

Speech Therapy (ST)

ST is a key component of the therapy services offered at CPFC, catering to individuals with speech and language disorders, communication difficulties associated with autism spectrum disorder, developmental delays, intellectual disabilities, and neurological conditions. Through a combination of exercises, techniques, and augmentative communication tools, ST aims to improve speech clarity, language comprehension, and social communication skills.

Behavioural Therapy

Behavioural therapy is utilised to support individuals with developmental disabilities, autism spectrum disorder, attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), and other behavioural or emotional disorders. By addressing challenging behaviours and teaching adaptive coping strategies, behavioural therapy helps students manage emotions, develop social skills, and achieve greater self-regulation.

Music Therapy

Music therapy offers a creative outlet for individuals with developmental disabilities, autism spectrum disorder, mental health disorders, and neurological conditions. Through music-based interventions — including singing, playing instruments, and rhythmic activities — music therapy promotes emotional expression, sensory integration, and social engagement.

Art Therapy

Art therapy provides a nonverbal means of expression for individuals with developmental disabilities, autism spectrum disorder, mental health disorders, and trauma-related conditions. By engaging in creative activities such as drawing, painting, and sculpting, art therapy encourages self-expression, enhances emotional resilience, and fosters self-awareness.

Aquatic Therapy

Aquatic therapy is particularly beneficial for individuals with physical disabilities such as cerebral palsy, spina bifida, muscular dystrophy, and orthopaedic conditions, as well as those with sensory processing disorders. Conducted in a pool environment, aquatic therapy utilises the buoyancy and resistance of water to improve mobility, strength, flexibility, and sensory integration.

Equine Therapy (Hippotherapy)

Equine therapy, also known as hippotherapy, is utilised to support individuals with physical disabilities, developmental disabilities, autism spectrum disorder, and mental health disorders. Through interactions with horses and equine-assisted activities, hippotherapy promotes physical, emotional, and cognitive development, while fostering trust, confidence, and self-esteem.

Recreational Therapy

Recreational therapy offers individuals with disabilities opportunities to engage in meaningful leisure and recreational activities tailored to their interests and abilities. By promoting active participation in sports, games, and outdoor pursuits, recreational therapy enhances physical fitness, social skills, and overall well-being.

Sensory Integration Therapy

Sensory integration therapy is primarily used for individuals with sensory processing disorders, autism spectrum disorder, developmental disabilities, and other neurological conditions affecting sensory processing and regulation. Through structured sensory experiences and activities, sensory integration therapy helps individuals develop adaptive responses to sensory input, improve sensory modulation, and enhance participation in daily activities.
