Welcome to CPFC

The Cerebral Palsy Foundation Centre (CPFC) is a special needs school and foundation based in Tudor, Mombasa, Kenya. We provide comprehensive education, therapy, and community support to children with cerebral palsy and other disabilities, including blindness, deafness, and mutism.

By contributing to CPFC, donors and supporters play a vital role in ensuring that individuals with diverse needs receive the necessary resources, therapies, and assistance programmes to navigate life's challenges and reach their full potential.

Our Vision

A world where children with special needs have their abilities, talents, and interests recognised and nurtured to enhance independence, productiveness, and acceptance in the community.

Our Mission

To nurture all children with special needs in a safe, inclusive, supportive environment through education, therapeutic intervention, and community acceptance.

Our Community

We organise events, workshops, and community-building activities where individuals with disabilities can come together, share experiences, and access support networks.

What We Do

CPFC's commitment to building an inclusive community is evident in its efforts to foster social connections and empower individuals of all abilities. Through organised events, workshops, and community-building activities, CPFC creates spaces where individuals with disabilities can come together, share experiences, and access support networks.

Advocacy lies at the heart of CPF's mission — we support efforts to raise awareness, advocate for policy changes, and combat discrimination against people with disabilities. Our empowerment programmes equip individuals with the skills and resources they need to live independently and achieve their goals.
